Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common Qurbani questions

What animals are valid for Qurbani? +
According to Islamic law, valid Qurbani animals include goats, sheep, cows, bulls, buffalo, and camels. Goats and sheep must be at least 1 year old (or 6 months if they appear mature). Cattle must be at least 2 years old. Camels must be at least 5 years old. All our animals meet these requirements and are verified by Islamic scholars.
Is your slaughter method Shariah-compliant? +
Yes, absolutely. Our slaughtermen are trained Muslims who follow the authentic Sunnah method. Bismillah and Allahu Akbar are recited, the animal faces Qibla, and a sharp blade is used for a swift cut of the throat, windpipe, and blood vessels. We follow guidelines accepted by Hanafi, Shafi'i, Maliki, and Hanbali schools of Islamic jurisprudence.
Can I see the animal before purchasing? +
Yes! We offer live video viewing via WhatsApp or Zoom. Simply contact us after selecting an animal, and our farm staff will show you the animal walking, from multiple angles, and answer any questions you have. This service is free and highly recommended.
How accurate is the weight measurement? +
We use calibrated digital scales for all weight measurements and provide a weight certificate with every purchase. You can also request video evidence of the weighing process. We offer a 100% money-back guarantee if there's any weight discrepancy exceeding 2-3 kg (normal variation due to feeding/water intake).
Can I book from overseas? +
Absolutely! Many overseas Pakistanis use our service to book Qurbani for their families back home. We accept international payments via bank transfer, credit/debit cards, and popular payment gateways. You'll receive complete updates via WhatsApp throughout the process, including photos and videos of slaughter and meat distribution.
What is included in the price? +
Our prices include the animal, complete veterinary health certificate, weight verification, and free delivery within city limits. If you choose our slaughter service, that's also included (unlike many providers who charge extra). Additional services like special cutting, extra packaging, or outstation delivery may have nominal charges, which are clearly stated upfront.
How does the charity meat donation work? +
When you select the charity distribution option, we coordinate with verified Islamic welfare organizations to deliver meat portions to deserving families identified through mosque committees and social welfare departments. You can choose to donate 1/3 (as per Sunnah), 1/2, or a custom portion. You'll receive photo/video evidence of the distribution along with a receipt for your records.
What if the animal gets sick before Eid? +
In the unlikely event that a booked animal becomes sick or unfit for Qurbani, we immediately inform you and provide two options: (1) Select an equivalent or better replacement animal at no extra cost, or (2) Receive a full refund. Our animals are kept in excellent conditions and regularly monitored by veterinarians to prevent such situations.
Can one large animal count for multiple people? +
Yes, according to Islamic law, one cow, bull, buffalo, or camel can be shared by up to 7 people, provided each person intends Qurbani and pays an equal share. We can help coordinate shared Qurbani bookings. Simply contact our team to discuss arrangements.
What are your payment methods? +
We accept bank transfers, credit/debit cards, JazzCash, Easypaisa, and international payment methods for overseas customers. You can also pay 50% advance and 50% on delivery. All payment details are provided during checkout, and your payment information is secure.
